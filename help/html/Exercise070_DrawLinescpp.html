Chapter 01, Section 02, Exercise 070_DrawLines.cpp


In this exercise, you will learn:


● How to draw lines on your drawing area using the fwcDrawLine() function.

● What parameters the fwcDrawLine()function takes.

● What a pixel is.

● What the abbreviation px means.


To do this exercise you need to know:


● How to load the fwc project in Qt Creator, and run it.

● What functions and parameters are.  ( See Exercise 010_ChangeText.cpp )


070_DrawLines.cpp draws three lines on your drawing area.  You need to position your three lines look to look like the lines in the SOLN.


Here is the first line of code, preceded by the USAGE comment for the fwcDrawLine() function:

   // USAGE: fwcDrawLine(xStart, yStart, xEnd, yEnd, color, linewidth);
   fwcDrawLine(0, 200, 400,   0, BLUE, 1);


So fwcDrawLine() is expecting you to specify two coordinate pairs to describe where you want the line drawn on the screen:  the starting point of the line, located by xStart,yStart, and the end point of the line, located by xEnd,yEnd.  The function call after the comment will draw a line from a starting point of 0,200 to an end point of 400,0.


The next parameter after these x,y coordinates is the color of the line, followed by the linewidth of the line in pixels.  (Throughout fwc you will see the word pixel abbreviated to px.)  A pixel is a dot on the screen, and is the smallest thing you can draw.  A pixel has an x,y location and a color.  With the fwc drawing tools, we mostly draw shapes on the screen like lines, circles and rectangles, and while some exercises will draw pixels, we usually don't draw individual pixels.


Go ahead and play around with the numbered parameters xStart,yStart and xEnd,yEnd. Make your changes and run your program to see if you can get your lines to be in the same position and orientation as the SOLN lines.  Leave the color and linewidth parameters alone until your lines match up with those of the SOLN.

When you are done working on the exercise, or if you get stuck and can't figure it out, check the solution code, which lives one directory below your exercise code in the "Solutions" directory, and is named:

● 070_DrawLinesSOLN.cpp

Always understand the SOLN code before you proceed to the next exercise!


After you achieve the solution here, feel free to change the color and linewidth parameters to your hearts content to see what they do!


When you are ready, you can proceed to 080_DrawCircles.cpp.
